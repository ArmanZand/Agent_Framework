# Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC)

WACC is the firm-level required rate of return: a blend of the costs of debt and equity, weighted by their share of total capital.

## Formula

WACC = (E/V) × Re + (D/V) × Rd × (1 − T)

where:
- E = market value of equity, D = market value of debt, V = E + D
- Re = cost of equity (typically estimated via CAPM)
- Rd = cost of debt (yield to maturity on existing debt)
- T = effective tax rate (debt interest is tax-deductible, hence the after-tax adjustment)

## When to use

- Discount rate for firm-level DCF valuation
- Hurdle rate for projects of similar risk to the firm's existing operations
- Capital structure decisions

## Pitfalls

- Use market values, not book values, for E and D
- For project valuation, adjust WACC if project risk differs from the firm's
- Cost of equity (Re) is unobservable — CAPM is the standard estimator but inputs (beta, equity risk premium) are noisy
- Forgetting the (1 − T) adjustment overstates WACC
- WACC assumes a stable target capital structure; it's not appropriate during major restructurings
