SECTOR RESEARCH NOTE — SEMICONDUCTORS
Prepared: October 2024  |  Analyst: Internal Research

=== SECTOR OVERVIEW ===

The global semiconductor industry is projected to reach $697bn in revenue for 2024,
up approximately 16% from $601bn in 2023, driven by a sharp recovery in memory pricing
and continued demand growth in AI accelerators. The prior downturn (2022–2023) was
characterised by inventory corrections across consumer electronics and PC markets.

Market structure:
- AI accelerators / data centre GPUs: ~$140bn TAM (2024E), growing >40% CAGR to 2027
- Memory (DRAM + NAND): ~$130bn TAM, highly cyclical, recovering from 2023 trough
- Logic / foundry: dominated by TSMC (~60% of advanced node capacity)
- Analogue: fragmented, more defensive, less exposure to AI spending cycle
- Equipment: ASML (EUV monopoly), Applied Materials, Lam Research

=== DEMAND DRIVERS ===

Near-term (12–18 months):
1. AI infrastructure buildout — hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta) are each
   committing $40–60bn in capex for FY2024/25, majority towards GPU clusters.
2. Memory pricing recovery — DRAM spot prices up ~55% YTD from Jan 2024 trough.
3. Smartphone upgrade cycle — modest recovery after two years of contraction.

Medium-term (2–4 years):
1. Sovereign AI programmes — US, EU, Japan, India investing in domestic compute.
2. Automotive semiconductor content growth — EVs require ~2x silicon vs ICE vehicles.
3. Edge AI — inference chips in devices, industrial IoT.

Risk factors:
1. US–China export restrictions — further tightening could cut ~$5–8bn annual revenue
   for Nvidia alone; broader equipment restrictions affect ASML (China ~25% of 2023 revenue).
2. Capex digestion — if hyperscaler AI spending plateaus or disappoints, GPU demand
   could contract sharply.
3. Cyclicality — memory segments historically see -30% to -50% revenue declines in
   downturn years.
4. Concentration risk — TSMC manufactures >90% of chips below 5nm; geopolitical risk
   around Taiwan remains a tail risk.

=== POSITIONING FRAMEWORK ===

Overweight:
- ASML — EUV monopoly is structural; China headwind largely priced in
- TSMC — Irreplaceable manufacturing position; attractive relative valuation

Neutral:
- Nvidia — Exceptional business, but 35x forward P/E prices in continued perfection
- Broadcom — Good execution; VMware synergies taking time

Underweight / avoid:
- Intel — Foundry strategy execution risk; multiple years from competitive parity
- Memory pure-plays — High cyclicality; current upcycle pricing in already

=== KEY QUESTIONS FOR NEXT EARNINGS SEASON ===

1. Are hyperscaler GPU order cadences holding or starting to stretch out?
2. ASML bookings — is the Q3 2024 shortfall a one-quarter blip or trend change?
3. Memory: are contract DRAM prices following spot higher, or is spot running ahead?
4. TSMC N2 yield ramp — on track for 2025 HVM?
5. Intel 18A — any customer tape-outs announced?
