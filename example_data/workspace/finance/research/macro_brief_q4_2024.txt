MACRO BRIEF — Q4 2024 OUTLOOK
Internal Research  |  September 2024

=== GLOBAL GROWTH PICTURE ===

IMF World GDP forecast 2024: +3.2% (unchanged from April revision)
Dispersion across regions remains high.

US:
- GDP growth tracking ~2.7% for 2024, above consensus entering the year.
- Labour market softening but not deteriorating: unemployment 4.2% (Aug 2024).
- PCE inflation: 2.5% YoY (Jul 2024), trending toward 2% target.
- Fed cut 50bps in September 2024. Market pricing ~150bps total cuts by end 2025.
- Soft landing appears most likely scenario (~60% probability).

Eurozone:
- GDP growth ~0.8% for 2024. Germany in technical recession.
- ECB cut twice in 2024 (Jun, Sep). More cuts expected.
- Inflation: 2.2% (Sep 2024), near target.
- Structural issues: energy costs, industrial competitiveness, fiscal fragmentation.
- Biggest drag: auto sector transition and loss of cheap Russian energy permanently.

China:
- GDP growth ~4.8% for 2024, below 5% official target.
- Property sector crisis ongoing; Evergrande liquidated Feb 2024.
- Deflation risk: CPI at 0.4%, PPI -1.8% (Aug 2024).
- Stimulus: PBOC cut RRR and LPR rates; fiscal stimulus announced Sep 2024 (~$280bn).
- Recovery uncertain; domestic consumption weak despite policy support.

UK:
- GDP growth ~1.1% for 2024. Recovery from 2023 stagnation.
- Bank of England cut in Aug 2024 (first since 2020); cautious easing path.
- Inflation sticky: services CPI 5.6%.

Japan:
- GDP growth ~0.4% for 2024.
- BoJ hiking for the first time in decades; JPY volatility elevated.
- Core inflation 2.0%; wage growth finally positive in real terms.

Emerging Markets:
- India: +7.0%, structural growth story intact.
- Brazil: +2.2%, commodity-supported.
- EM broadly: benefit from falling USD and Fed cuts, but idiosyncratic risks persist.

=== TAIL RISKS FOR Q4 2024 ===

1. US election outcome (Nov 2024) — tariff escalation under either outcome possible;
   higher tariff scenario negative for global trade, EM, and European exports.
2. Middle East escalation — oil supply disruption risk; Brent spike to $100+ tail.
3. China stimulus disappointment — if announced measures don't translate to demand,
   commodity cycle and EM equities face renewed selling.
4. Japan policy error — over-rapid BoJ tightening could unwind yen carry trades
   (estimated ~$4trn) causing cross-asset volatility spike.
5. Credit event — leveraged buyout market stress as refinancing walls approach 2025/26.

=== ASSET ALLOCATION IMPLICATIONS ===

Equities: neutral. Valuations full in US (S&P 500 fwd P/E ~21x). Europe/EM cheaper.
Fixed income: constructive on duration. Real yields positive; cutting cycle supportive.
Gold: hold. Central bank demand structural; performs well in rate-cut environments.
Cash: reduce as rates fall; deploy into fixed income ladder.
Alternatives: infrastructure and real assets attractive for inflation hedging.
